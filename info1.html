Basic Info

Black holes are formed from mass bursts of energy, and stay alive on said energy for unbelievable amounts of time. They absorb and pull in everything surrounding them to consume, including, gas, planets, and even light. Since they consume so much they are the heaviest things in the universe. The matter they consume is condensed infinitely into the center called a 'singularity'. They are invisible, but can be detected due to the lack of stars, light, and gas, or their gravitational affect on other celestial bodies. They push what we know about physics and science and remains one of the biggest universal mysteries.


Anatomy

Hover and press anywhere on the black hole to learn more!

Singularity

The center of the black hole, where everything is crushed to infinite density.

Accretion Disk

The remnants of anything being sucked in, that has yet to be sucked in, such as light, swirl around, forming the accretion disk.

Photon Ring

A secondary thin ring of light, that has not been sucked in, but is closer.

Event Horizon

Nearing the center, the boundary where if you need to escape, you need to be faster than light.

Warped Light

The backside of the black hole shows light warping from the front due to its gravitational pull. It is essentially light being bent into our view.

Doppler Beaming

An effect where the light accumulated by the black hole is brighter towards us, but dimmer as it farthers.

Life Cycle

Black holes can form in many ways. However it always occurs when things merge, like massive stars or multiple smaller black holes. When large entities like stars or suns die, they let out massive spikes of energy, called supernovas. Those spikes of energy have a possibility of forming black holes. But due to how long they live, many black holes are pre-existing and grow over time. Other than those 2 methods, how they form is widely unknown.



Black holes exist purely to absorb and destroy matter. They are capable of even destroying galaxies. They are the only way known to actually destroy matter, as it gets pulled in and crushed, with no idea of what it is like on the other side. Black holes form with collapses of star so all they do is pull things in to consume and get bigger.



Black holes can live longer than anything else in this universe. They only die by slowly losing energy over their lifetime. Which can take 10 to the power of 100 years. They slowly let out small amounts of particles at a time until eventually after googols (10¹⁰⁰ years) of years, when it is low on energy. And when it is low, it detonates with a massive nuclear power much, much more than all we have on earth, then disappears.
